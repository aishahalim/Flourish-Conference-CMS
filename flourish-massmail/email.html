About

Flourish conference is UIC's sixth annual conference held at Student Center East to promote the use and adoption of Free Libre Open Source Software (FLOSS) by demonstrating the practical applications of FLOSS in the business and academic world.

What

UIC Students, friends and family are invited to come to Flourish Conference to come to learn about how Free and Open Source software can improve their lives. Attendees do not have to have a strong technological background as the conference is aimed to be a high level overview of open source.

Featured Speakers and Topics

Flourish! 2012 Speakers include:
  • Brian Fitzpatrick, a proponent of Data Liberation from Google
  • Bryen M. Yunashko, a prominent figure from the OpenSuse project
  • Chris McAvoy, working with Mozilla's Open Badges project
  • Meg Ford, on Gnome outreach and involvement in GSoC
  • Jason Huggins, of Selenium and SauceLabs fame, talking about robots!
  • Phil Gomes, Edelman, speaking addressing the outcome of corporate involvement and Wikipedia
  • ...and more.

Featured Workshop

Get some hands-on experience with soldering and electronics at our Arduino Workshop. For the first time, Flourish! Conference has partnered with Pumping Station: One to provide a workshop for beginners to learn how to program Arduino chips. Arduino has been used to create some cool projects ranging from a miniature pocket piano to an RGB combination door lock!
